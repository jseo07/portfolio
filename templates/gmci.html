Generative Adversarial Network for Composite Generation

This project, submitted to the International Science and Engineering Fair (ISEF) 2021, explores and optimizes the latent space and noise vectors of pre-existing Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) to achieve refined and meaningful composite image generation. Instead of designing or training new GAN architectures, I leveraged existing models to investigate how adjustments in latent space and noise vectors influence the generated outputs.

Understanding and Manipulating Latent Space

The latent space of GANs serves as the creative foundation for generating outputs. By systematically analyzing this space, I identified key patterns and dimensions that influence specific visual characteristics of the generated images. Using these insights, I developed techniques to manipulate the latent vectors, enabling controlled generation of images with desired attributes.

Optimizing Noise Vectors for Better Outputs

Noise vectors play a crucial role in the randomness and diversity of GAN outputs. I experimented with different distributions and scaling techniques for the noise vectors, optimizing their initialization to enhance the quality and variety of generated composites. This process provided a deeper understanding of how GANs interpret and translate noise into meaningful features.

Results and Applications

The optimizations yielded significantly improved image quality and consistency, demonstrating the potential for controlled and customized generation in GANs. This project has practical applications in fields like art generation, synthetic data creation, and design automation, showcasing how latent space exploration can bridge creative intent with technical execution.

Impact and Takeaways

By focusing on optimization within the latent space and noise vectors, this project underscored the importance of parameter tuning and creative exploration in using GANs effectively. The submission to ISEF 2021 was a valuable experience, allowing me to present my work on an international stage and receive feedback from experts in science and engineering. It enhanced my skills in working with advanced machine learning models and deepened my understanding of generative techniques, inspiring me to further investigate the transformative potential of GANs in creative and technical domains.

This experience has solidified my passion for machine learning and its intersection with creativity, motivating me to explore innovative ways to apply generative models to solve real-world challenges.
